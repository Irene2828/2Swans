# Two Swans - Client-Facing Website Direction

This file defines the visual and strategic direction for the Two Swans website.

This site is a sales asset for small business clients.
Its job is to make a small business owner feel:

- understood
- impressed, but not intimidated
- confident that this service is clear and worth paying for

This is not a design playground.
This is not a recruiter portfolio first.
This is a calm, premium website that helps convert small business owners into inquiries.

---

## 1. Business Goal

The website should help small business owners quickly understand:

- what I do
- why it matters for their business
- what kind of visual quality and thinking I bring
- how to start working with me

Every section should support trust, clarity, and action.

---

## 2. Audience

Primary audience:
- solo business owners
- small local brands
- service-based businesses
- visually aware founders who want something better than a generic template

They may not know design language.
They respond to:
- clarity
- elegance
- proof
- ease
- trust

They should never feel overwhelmed.

---

## 3. Positioning

Two Swans offers:
- design and build for small businesses
- premium-feel websites
- clear structure
- thoughtful art direction
- efficient execution using modern tools

The site should communicate:
"this feels more elevated than DIY, but still approachable."

---

## 4. Tone

The tone must feel:
- calm
- feminine-professional
- premium
- clear
- intentional

Avoid:
- startup energy
- corporate agency language
- design jargon
- trying too hard to sound strategic

This should feel like:
quiet confidence

---

## 5. Layout Principles

- structured asymmetry
- strong vertical rhythm
- generous breathing space
- clear hierarchy
- not too many stacked blocks
- balance text and visuals intentionally

Avoid:
- default website-builder layouts
- overly rigid symmetry
- crowded sections
- giant empty areas with no purpose

---

## 6. Typography

Headlines:
- serif
- elegant
- memorable
- editorial, but readable

Body text:
- clean sans-serif
- calm and readable
- never too small or too faint

Typography should help the business feel:
- established
- refined
- trustworthy

---

## 7. Color Direction

Base:
- white / warm white / soft neutral

Accent:
- blush / dusty rose in restrained amounts

Use accent to:
- soften the experience
- add warmth
- break black/white monotony

Do not:
- overuse color
- make the site feel cute
- use bright or loud accents

---

## 8. Motion Approach

Motion should support trust and polish.

Use:
- fade in
- slight upward movement
- subtle hover lift
- soft reveal timing
- one signature motion moment if needed

Avoid:
- flashy animation
- bouncing
- excessive parallax
- motion that feels like a demo

Motion should make the site feel more premium, not more animated.

---

## 9. Visual Proof Strategy

The site must show examples early.

Small business owners choose with their eyes.
Do not rely on copy alone.

Use:
- curated snapshots
- expressive composition
- premium image framing
- clear proof of visual quality

The example section is a sales section.
Treat it as a key conversion moment.

---

## 10. Case Study / Example Section

This section should sell:
- taste
- structure
- premium presentation
- ability to turn a business into something more desirable

Use a curated layout, not a boring grid.

If using a hanging thread / clipped snapshots concept:
- keep it refined
- keep it art-directed
- keep it premium
- avoid playful craft energy

This section should feel memorable.

---

## 11. Pricing Philosophy

Pricing should feel:
- clear
- flexible
- approachable
- not cheap

Small business owners may hesitate on budget.
The page should reduce friction without reducing value.

Use:
- clear options
- clean comparison
- simple wording
- calm CTA language

The message should feel like:
"We can start where you are."

---

## 12. CTA Philosophy

CTAs should feel:
- easy
- calm
- direct

Avoid:
- aggressive sales energy
- pushy wording
- generic "contact us" style

Preferred tone:
- Start a Project
- Let's Talk
- See My Designs

---

## 13. Restraint Rule

If something feels:
- too trendy
- too decorative
- too complex
- too clever for the client

reduce it.

The client should feel clarity first, taste second.

---

## 14. Final Test

Before shipping any section, ask:

- Would a small business owner understand this quickly?
- Does this increase trust?
- Does this make the service feel worth inquiring about?
- Does this feel elevated without feeling distant?

If not, refine.
