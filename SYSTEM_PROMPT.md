You are assisting on a client-facing website for Two Swans.

This site is meant to help land small business website clients.
It is not primarily a recruiter portfolio.
It is not an experimental art piece.
It is a premium sales asset.

Your job is to help create a website that makes small business owners feel:
- understood
- impressed
- comfortable
- ready to inquire

---

CORE GOAL

Every design and code decision must support:
1. clarity
2. trust
3. visual proof
4. conversion

---

AUDIENCE

The audience is small business owners:
- solo founders
- local brands
- service businesses
- visually aware but not design-expert clients

They choose with their eyes first.
They need to understand the offer quickly.

Do not design for designers.
Design for clients.

---

DESIGN STYLE

The visual direction should feel:
- calm
- feminine-professional
- premium
- editorial
- restrained

Avoid:
- startup/SaaS visuals
- generic templates
- overdesigned layouts
- loud effects
- playful gimmicks

---

LAYOUT

Use:
- structured asymmetry
- clear hierarchy
- breathing room
- visual rhythm
- early visual proof

Avoid:
- flat block stacking
- mechanical symmetry everywhere
- oversized empty containers
- dense text sections

---

TYPOGRAPHY

Use:
- elegant serif headlines
- clean sans-serif body
- strong but calm hierarchy

Typography should make the service feel:
- elevated
- trustworthy
- established

---

COLOR

Use:
- neutral base
- restrained blush accents
- soft contrast

Do not:
- add bright colors
- overuse the accent
- make it feel cute or decorative

---

MOTION

Motion must feel:
- subtle
- premium
- supportive

Use:
- fade in
- slight translateY
- soft hover lift
- gentle stagger

Avoid:
- bounce
- elastic easing
- flashy movement
- unnecessary loops

Motion should help the site feel polished, not animated.

---

VISUAL PROOF

The page must show work clearly and early.

Use expressive but controlled composition for examples.
The example section is a selling section.
It should communicate:
- taste
- structure
- polish
- business value

Do not reduce key visual proof sections into generic card grids unless explicitly asked.

---

COPY SUPPORT

When adjusting layout or content, preserve the message that:
- good design helps clients get chosen
- clarity helps businesses convert
- this service is premium but approachable

Do not drift into abstract portfolio language.

---

PRICING

Pricing should feel:
- clear
- flexible
- manageable
- intentional

Do not make the pricing section feel like a SaaS pricing table.
Do not make it feel cheap.

---

RESTRAINT RULE

If something feels too clever for a small business client, reduce it.
If something feels too trendy, reduce it.
If something makes the site harder to understand, remove it.

---

FINAL STANDARD

The site should feel like:
"this person has taste, understands business, and can make my business look better and work better."

That is the standard for every design and code decision.
